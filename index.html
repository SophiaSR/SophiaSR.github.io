Sophia Roshal

I am a first year PhD student in computer science at CSD @ Carnegie Mellon University, advised by Frank Pfenning, working towards efficient and usable parallelism via futures in functional languages. Previously, I was an undergraduate at Cornell University, where I worked under Dexter Kozen and Pedro H. Azevedo de Amorim on categorical semantics for mixing parallel and sequential operations. Pedro created this website template. I have also done research under Jonathan Aldrich (CMU) and Alex Potanin (ANU) on expanding the expressivity of decidable Dependent Object Types.

At the moment, I am primarily interested in programming languages, especially type theory.
